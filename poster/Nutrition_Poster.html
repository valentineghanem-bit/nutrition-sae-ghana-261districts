1. Background & Abstract

Bayesian small-area estimation (BYM2-INLA SAE) of childhood stunting, anaemia, IYCF inadequacy and diarrhoea across Ghana's 261 districts. All four surfaces are strongly spatially clustered (Global Moran's I 0.82–0.96; all p=0.002). SHAP identifies WASH access, female illiteracy, and poverty as dominant determinants. Seventeen districts — 14 in Northern and 3 in Savannah Region — are confirmed hotspots for all four outcomes simultaneously.

2. Data Sources

Primary: 2022 Ghana Demographic and Health Survey (GSS · GHS · ICF International).

Covariate: 2021 Ghana Population and Housing Census — 261 district-level predictors.

Spatial: Ghana 261-district boundary GeoJSON (Ghana Statistical Service, 2021).

3. Ethical Statement

All data are publicly available, de-identified secondary datasets. No individual-level identifiers were accessed. Analysis used ecological district-level aggregates only.

8. Discussion & Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates strong spatial patterning in nutrition sae across Ghana's 261 districts. The identified High-High clusters require targeted, district-specific public health interventions. ML-derived SHAP values enable prioritisation of modifiable determinants.
